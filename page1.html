MOVERE AD IMAGINEM


Ravenstein is located near the ‘Kunstberg’. ‘Kunstberg’ is a historic site in the center of Brussels. It is the art district of the city. Ravenstein isn’t connected to art. It is a misplaced shopping gallery and is now only just as a passage between the central station and the Bozar. I want to give the gallery a new function that is connected to the diffetent film art schools in Brussels. 



Movere ad imaginem what literally means 'the moving image'. My project is a consideration of a collective working space for artists focused on cinematography. 

People tell stories and those stories are often depicted. Imagining those stories we call cinematography. Today it is hard for small free film makers to compete against the large film industry. There are free film makers or graduates who have little or no budget to produce a movie. Film schools like RITS, LUCA school of arts, Narafi haven't enough place for students to produce, record, show, etc. I have fixed myself on this problem. I created a place for artists and collectives where they get the opportunity to produce and show their films. 

The design is simplistic and made up of two assembly rooms and eight cinema rooms. The design of the rooms are made up of inclined walls and a sloping ceiling.The main structure is in metal. The materials of the construction are marine plywood, transparent and semi transparent glass. The seats consist of pullout benches. 

The eight cinema rooms are in an asymmetrical but thoughtful way placed in the space. By that assymetrical strain you experience a difference in ups and downs when you enter the space. The rooms are multifuntional and made of a simple construction so they can be transformated in recording studios or rooms for scenography.
